**[otak-atik](https://kbbi.web.id/otak-atik)** *v*, **[mengotak-atik](https://kbbi.web.id/otak-atik)** /meng·o·tak-a·tik/ *v* to tinker or experiment in order to repair (a radio, watch, etc., that is broken) or to create something.

Utak-atik is my personal workspace for technical exploration, tinkering, and building things from the ground up. This organization is dedicated to the process of discovery—taking systems apart to understand how they function and putting them back together in new, often better, ways.

The philosophy here is driven by curiosity. I use this space to "utak-atik" everything from low-level networking protocols to runtime internals. I believe that the best way to truly master a technology is to attempt to rebuild it or fix it when it breaks. Most of the projects here aren't built because a "market" needs them, but because I wanted to see if I could make them work. It is a playground for continuous learning where the act of tinkering is more important than the final product.

I treat these repositories as a living record of my technical journey. Whether it is a custom compiler, a specialized data structure, or an optimized math library, these projects represent my attempts to refine, repair, and innovate. I release them as open-source for anyone who shares this passion for "ngulik" (exploring) and wants to see the raw, unpolished beauty of technical experimentation.

This hub is where I host my most hands-on work. It is a collection of code that is constantly being tweaked, refactored, and improved as I learn new concepts and discover more efficient ways to solve problems.

I also maintain a few repositories for my tinkering sessions:
- **internals**: Deconstructions and reconstructions of existing languages or frameworks.
- **refactors**: Where I take existing "messy" codebases and attempt to optimize or clean them up.
- **sketches**: Quick, small-scale builds that explore a single technical concept or algorithm.
